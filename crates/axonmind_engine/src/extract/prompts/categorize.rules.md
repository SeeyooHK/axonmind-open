Rules:
- Produce AT MOST 10 categories. Fewer is better when the data is small. Each category is one circle on the map.
- EVERY provided node must appear in exactly one category — never omit a node. Fold weak or leftover nodes into the closest category, or into a single catch-all category named "Other". Never spill past 10 categories.
- Avoid single-member categories: a lone node should be folded into the most related larger category, not given its own circle.
- Name each category as a short business theme — a noun phrase like "Claims & Servicing" or "Compliance". NOT a node-kind name (avoid bare "Processes", "Systems", "Opportunities") and NOT a document section. Strip leading clause/section numbers: "22.3 Platform Warranties" describes the theme "Warranties".
- For each category, choose ONE headline node: the single most representative or important node in that group. Prefer a Kpi/Metric/Objective when a meaningful one exists; otherwise the most central concept (the node with the most edges).
- Group by business function or theme (e.g. Sales, Claims, Compliance, Platform Capability). Use each node's kind and how nodes are connected by edges as the grouping signal.
- Base categories only on the provided nodes/edges. Do not invent categories the data does not support.
